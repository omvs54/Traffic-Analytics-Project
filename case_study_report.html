Traffic Optimization Case Study

Date: October 26, 2023

Prepared By: Data Analytics Team

1. Executive Summary

This report analyzes historical traffic sensor data to identify congestion patterns and propose data-driven solutions for traffic management. The analysis reveals critical bottlenecks at specific junctions during evening peak hours. By implementing targeted signal timing adjustments and infrastructure improvements, we project a potential 15-20% reduction in wait times.

2. Data Analysis & Methodology

The study utilized a dataset containing 48,120 hourly records across 4 major junctions. The following steps were taken:

  • Data Cleaning: Handled missing values via median imputation and standardized timestamps.
  • Modeling: Designed a Star Schema with Fact_Traffic and dimensions for Time and Location.
  • Enrichment: Integrated geospatial coordinates and simulated weather/accident data for deeper correlation analysis.

3. Key Insights

Insight 1: The "Evening Rush" Phenomenon

Traffic volume consistently peaks between 17:00 and 19:00, with volumes 40% higher than the daily average. A secondary, smaller peak occurs at 08:00.

Insight 2: Critical Hotspot at Junction 1

Junction 1 (Downtown/Main St) is the primary bottleneck, handling nearly double the traffic volume of Junction 4. It accounts for 35% of all recorded congestion events.

Insight 3: Weather Impact

Analysis indicates a correlation between rainy conditions and a 10% decrease in average speed, contributing to longer queue lengths, particularly at Junction 2.

4. Proposed Solutions

Based on the data, we recommend the following immediate and long-term actions:

Short-Term: Dynamic Signal Timing

Action: Extend green light duration for Junction 1 Northbound lanes between 17:00-19:00.

Expected Outcome: Alleviate the evening bottleneck without major infrastructure costs.

Medium-Term: Resource Allocation

Action: Deploy traffic marshals to Junction 1 and 2 during rainy weather forecasts to manage flow and prevent intersection blocking.

Long-Term: Infrastructure Review

Action: Conduct a feasibility study for widening the turn lane at Junction 1, as volume exceeds current lane capacity by 15% during peaks.

5. Conclusion

The data clearly points to specific time windows and locations where intervention yields the highest ROI. Transitioning from static management to a data-responsive approach will significantly improve commuter experience.
